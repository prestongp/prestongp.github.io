Nirvana

The Band That Changed Rock Forever

About the Band

Nirvana was an American rock band formed in 1987 in Aberdeen, Washington. The band became one of the most influential groups in rock history and helped popularize grunge music in the early 1990s.

Band Members

The classic lineup consisted of Kurt Cobain (vocals & guitar), Krist Novoselic (bass), and Dave Grohl (drums).

Famous Album

Their 1991 album Nevermind was a massive success. It featured the hit single “Smells Like Teen Spirit,” which became an anthem of a generation. Other albums include In Utero and Bleach.

Legacy

Nirvana’s raw sound, emotional lyrics, and anti-establishment attitude left a lasting impact on music and culture worldwide.
